Concept A: Side Panel

A collapsible panel slides open on the right. Both chats are visible simultaneously. The main analysis chat narrows to make room.

Assistant
I've analyzed your dataset sales_data.csv with 4 columns and 1,200 rows. Here's what I found...
[ Dataset summary cards ]
You
Show me revenue trends over time
Assistant
Here's a line chart showing sales trends over time:
[ Line chart: Sales over Time ]

Report an Issue

System
Describe what looks wrong. I'll note the issue and our team will look into it.
You
That chart is wrong — sales should be going up, not down
System
Thanks for flagging that. Were you expecting sales to increase over the full time range, or just in a specific period?
Interaction: Click "Report Issue" in the header to toggle the side panel open/closed. The main chat narrows when the panel is open. Both conversations are active simultaneously.

Pros

Both chats visible at once — user can reference the analysis output while reporting. No context switching. Feels like a natural "aside."

Cons

Main chat narrows, which may cause layout reflow on charts/tables. More complex responsive behavior. Two active input areas could confuse focus.

Concept B: Tab / Toggle

A tab bar below the header switches between "Analysis" and "Report Issue" views. Only one is visible at a time. Each has its own full-width chat area.

Assistant
I've analyzed your dataset sales_data.csv with 4 columns and 1,200 rows. Here's what I found...
[ Dataset summary cards ]
You
Show me revenue trends over time
Assistant
Here's a line chart showing sales trends over time:
[ Line chart: Sales over Time ]
You
Break it down by region
Assistant
Here's the regional breakdown:
[ Grouped bar chart: Sales by Region ]
System
Describe what looks wrong with the analysis or output. I'll note the issue and our team will look into it.
You
That chart is wrong — sales should be going up, not down
System
Thanks for flagging that. We've noted the issue and our team will look into it.
Interaction: Click the tabs to switch views. The red dot on "Report Issue" (visible when you switch back to Analysis after reporting) indicates an active or acknowledged report. Each tab has its own independent chat history and input.

Pros

Cleanest separation — main chat component untouched structurally. Full-width bug chat. Equally discoverable. Simple invariant: "Analysis tab is identical to current chat."

Cons

User can't see the analysis output while reporting. Must remember what was wrong and switch tabs. Adds a tab bar that takes vertical space from the chat.

Concept C: Modal / Drawer

A floating button opens a slide-out drawer from the right. The main chat is visible (dimmed) underneath. The drawer overlays rather than displacing content.

I've analyzed your dataset sales_data.csv with 4 columns and 1,200 rows. Here's what I found...

Report an Issue

System

You
That chart is wrong — sales should be going up, not down
System

Interaction: Click the "Report Issue" floating button (bottom-right) to open/close the drawer. The main chat is dimmed but still visible behind the overlay. Click the overlay or × to close.

Pros

Lowest risk to existing UI — main chat DOM is completely untouched. Drawer is a separate component tree. User can still see the analysis (dimmed) for reference while reporting.

Cons

Feels secondary/hidden — less discoverable than tabs. The floating button could overlap with content. Modal pattern implies a transient action, but bug reports may need revisiting.
